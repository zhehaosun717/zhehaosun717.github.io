## 2024-05-02 - Pre-calculating squared thresholds in WebGL hot loops
**Learning:** The background particle animation system recalculates the square of `connectionDistance` and `mouseDistance` thousands of times per frame in the `animate` loop (e.g., `distSq2 < connectionDistance * connectionDistance` and `distSq < mouseDistance * mouseDistance`). This causes unnecessary operations on the main thread for constant thresholds. Replacing repeated threshold multiplications with pre-calculated squared constants results in approximately a 7% performance improvement for the particle system's hot loop logic.
**Action:** When calculating Euclidean distance thresholds in high-frequency animation loops (like WebGL `requestAnimationFrame`), pre-calculate and store squared distance thresholds outside the loop instead of performing the multiplication or calling `Math.sqrt` on every iteration.
